Professional Experience

Zachtronics Game Programming Intern (Aug 2019 - Jan 2020)

I spent 6 months as a game programming intern at Zachtronics (wikipedia) where I built gameplay and engine features.

Zachtronics is an indie game studio that has been making unique programming puzzle games since 2000. The genre of their games is often called "Zach-Like" due to the consistency and quality of the studio's corpus.

My job entailed working both independently and with the team to define and implement game features.

Most of my time was spent working on Mobius Front, a 2D top-down tactics game set in the 1980's.

I had ownership of unit art integration. This entailed programming the gameplay animation system, working with the artists to ensure the animations in-game matched their expectations, and building debugging tools to facilitate communication.

I also spent much of my time implementing gameplay logic and unit pathfinding.

I had the unique opportunity to design and build a procedurally generated UI animation in MOLEK-SYNTEZ, a 2D top-down programming puzzle game about synthesizing molecules from atoms.
